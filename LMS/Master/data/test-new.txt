1)	When is International Day of the World's Indigenous Peoples observed every year?

	a)	5 August

	b)	7 August

	c)	8 August

	d)	9 August

	e)	10 August



The correct answer is option <b>4 </b>i.e. <b><u>9 August</u></b><b>:</b>



--	The United Nations’ (UN) <b>International Day of the World's Indigenous People</b> is observed on <b><u>August 9</u></b> each year to promote and protect the rights of the world’s indigenous population.

--	On <b>23 December 1994</b>, the United Nations General Assembly decided, in its resolution 49/214, that the International Day of the World's Indigenous People shall be observed on 9 August every year.

--	The date marks the day of the first meeting, in 1982, of the UN Working Group on Indigenous Populations.

--	This year’s theme is <b>COVID-19 and indigenous peoples’ resilience</b>.

--	It will focus on how preservation and promotion of indigenous peoples’ traditional knowledge and practices.

--	Indigenous Peoples are culturally distinct societies and communities.

--	There are approximately 476 million Indigenous Peoples worldwide, in over 90 countries.
